Zixuan Ke

I am a 4th-year PhD student at the University of Illinois, Chicago, where I am advised by Prof. Bing Liu. Prior to that, I received my M.Sc. in Computer Science from the University of Texas, Dallas and was advised by Prof. Vincent Ng. I am broadly interested in Machine Learning and Natural Language Processing. I have closely worked with Hu Xu from Meta AI and Lei Shu from Google Research. During my Ph.D., I worked as a research intern at Meta AI, Amazon AI and Tencent AI Lab.

As a basic researcher, I work on continual and lifelong learning (NeurIPS20, 21, 22)

As an applied researcher, I work on natural language processing including LM pre-training (EMNLP22), continual LM pre-training (EMNLP22), continual text classification (NAACL21, EMNLP21), and argument mining (ACL18,19; IJCAI18,19).

My long-term goal is to develop more general and intelligent systems (AGI), which are able to accumulate knowledge over time and leverage the past learned knowledge to enable better future learning.
